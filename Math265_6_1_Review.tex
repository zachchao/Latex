\subsection*{Review for 6.1 - Laplace Transform}
The Laplace transform is defined as 
$$\laplace\{f(t)\} = F(s) = \int_0^\infty e^{-st} f(t) dt$$
This just means that the Laplace of a function is the improper integral of the product of $e^{-st}$ and the function, where s is a constant.\\

\subsubsection*{Example - Find the Laplace of $f(t) = t$}
By our definition $\laplace\{t\} = \int_0^\infty e^{-st}t dt$\\
So we need only solve the integral, it is clear that this is done through integration by parts.\\
$$
\begin{tabular}{l l}
 let $u = t$ & let $dv = e^{-st}$\\
 $du = dt$ & $v = \frac{-e^{-st}}{s}$
\end{tabular}
$$
$$\lim_{A \to \infty} \int_0^A e^{-st}t dt = \lim_{A \to \infty} \frac{-te^{-st}}{s}\Big|_0^A - \int_0^A \frac{-e^{-st}}{s} dt$$
